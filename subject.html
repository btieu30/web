About UMI

Tierra Wilson, better known as UMI, was born in Seattle to an African-American father and Japanese mother. Her stage name, UMI, is her Japanese middle name, which translates to "ocean". The 21 year-old is well-known for her ability to blend unique music genres such as R&B, neo-soul and lo-fi music to make hits. UMI officially began her career in 2017, with the song "Happy Again", which has garnered over 3 million plays on the streaming platform known as Spotify and 400,000 views on Youtube. UMI's Rise To Fame

UMI started out as a regular high school student, who enjoyed making covers for leisure. Soon, these covers started getting copyrighted and she began writing her own songs. Once she began attending college at the University of Southern California, it was difficult for her to manage school and music, so she dropped out of college. UMI truly began her rise to fame in 2018, with the hit song "Remember Me", which has over 26 million views on Youtube. Listen to the audio for "Remember Me" below:
